Who are we

High velocity flame spraying is often also referred to as HVOF spraying (high velocity oxy-fuel spraying). The method, which has developed rapidly in recent years, produces a high velocity of the molten particles, giving a very strong, dense layer, with less than 1 % porosity and low oxide inclusion.

Fuel gases may be paraffin, LPG or hydrogen, burnt with oxygen at high pressure in a combustion chamber. Temperatures of 2700–3100 °C are reached, depending on the fuel gas.
